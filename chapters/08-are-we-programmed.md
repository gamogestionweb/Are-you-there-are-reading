## The Libet Experiment

<table>
<tr>
<td width="100" align="center">
<h2>1983</h2>
<sub>Benjamin Libet</sub>
</td>
<td>

Experiments in [neuroscience](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuroscience) suggest that our brain makes decisions **before we're conscious of them**.

Based on [Benjamin Libet's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Libet) work, it was discovered that brain scans could predict a person's decision up to **seven seconds** before they believed they had made it.

## Behavioral Patterns

If we observe our behavior, our routines, our automatic responses, we might start to notice something unsettling:

<p align="center">
<strong>We follow patterns.</strong>
</p>

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Daily routines | Same coffee, same route, same time |
| Emotional reactions | Anger triggers, comfort behaviors |
| Relationship dynamics | Repeating the same conflicts |
| Decision shortcuts | "I always choose X" |
| Thought loops | The same worries, over and over |

> *As we saw in [Chapter VI: Peer Pressure](06-peer-pressure.md), social pressure amplifies this "programming"*

## The Paradox

Even if we are programmed:
- The act of questioning the programming might itself be programmed
- Or it might be the one truly free act we can perform
- Or the distinction might not matter at all

**What matters is that you're asking the question.**
