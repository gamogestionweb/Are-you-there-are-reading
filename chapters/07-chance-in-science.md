# Chapter VII
## Chance in Science
### Discoveries That Shouldn't Have Happened

<p align="center">
  <em>"The history of science is full of discoveries that seemed to emerge from nowhere"</em>
</p>

---

The history of science is full of discoveries that seemed to emerge from nowhere—moments when chance played a crucial role.

---

## The Accidental Discoveries

### LSD

<table>
<tr>
<td width="100" align="center">
<h2>1938</h2>
</td>
<td>

**[Albert Hofmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Hofmann)** was trying to synthesize a circulatory stimulant.

By accident, he absorbed a minimal amount of the compound through his fingertips.

What he experienced changed the course of neuroscience and culture forever: [LSD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LSD).

</td>
</tr>
</table>

---

### Velcro

<table>
<tr>
<td width="100" align="center">
<h2>1941</h2>
</td>
<td>

**[George de Mestral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_de_Mestral)** went for a walk with his dog.

He noticed how [burdock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctium) seeds stuck stubbornly to his clothes and his dog's fur.

Under a microscope, he discovered tiny hooks—and invented [Velcro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Velcro).

</td>
</tr>
</table>

---

### X-Rays

<table>
<tr>
<td width="100" align="center">
<h2>1895</h2>
</td>
<td>

**[Wilhelm Röntgen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilhelm_R%C3%B6ntgen)** was experimenting with [cathode ray tubes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathode-ray_tube).

He noticed a fluorescent screen glowing across the room—even though the tube was covered.

He had discovered [X-rays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X-ray), revolutionizing medicine forever.

</td>
</tr>
</table>

## Complete List of Accidental Discoveries

| Discovery | Year | Scientist | The Accident |
|-----------|------|-----------|--------------|
| [Penicillin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penicillin) | 1928 | [Alexander Fleming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Fleming) | Mold contaminated his petri dish |

| [Velcro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Velcro) | 1941 | [George de Mestral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_de_Mestral) | Burdock seeds on clothes |
| [Microwave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microwave_oven) | 1945 | [Percy Spencer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percy_Spencer) | Chocolate melted in pocket |
